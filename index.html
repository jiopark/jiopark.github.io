Adaptable App



This was an app created for CITRIS' (Center for Information Technology Research in the Interest of Society) Mobile App Challenge. We were given 6 months to create an app that would positively affect society using current technology.
As acting design lead I worked on branding and UX/UI design.

The app is a natural disaster calculating app, that pulls local climate data in order to predict the user's risk of regional natural disasters. It draws information from NASA and NOAA's public databases, along with reputable sources on social media.

The app ended up winning first place in the competition, and was offered funding for further development.

Front page of the app. Below is a rough disaster preparation checklist, as this app not only calculated a user's risk but also provided information on how to be prepared and what to do during said disaster. Natural disasters such as drought, floods, and earthquakes were taken into consideration.

On the user profile page is a progress chart that rewards users for completing a disaster-specific preparation checklist, and let's the user know what percent they are prepared. Eventual additions to the app included sharing your information with other users, so that you could monitor climate conditions for friends, family and loved ones who live in different regions.

  • Client: CITRIS
  • Date: June 2015
  • Service: UI/UX, Branding

Treadmill UI Redesign



With the increased interest in UX/UI in technology, I found there was a gap in development when it came to Treadmill UI design. I decided to try and redesign the UX/UI for the LifeFitness Treadmills that were the most common at local gyms.


Research included studying the current UX/UI, as well as visiting local gyms and surveying 40 gym goers about what they liked/disliked about the treadmills.

The current UI is outdated, skeuomorphic and clunky. It's difficult to use and offers too many options on one screen.


Early drafts of the 'Create workout' screen, the work out screen, views 1 and 2. The plan was to have it be entirely touch screen, rather than physical buttons.


I wanted to create an interface that was not only reliable, safe and intuitive to use but also inspirational and beautiful. I wanted an interface that was friendlier and encouraged users to reach their fitness goals.

One objective was to take the user from adding their information to starting their workout as soon as possible.

There is an optional 'scenic mode,' that allows the user to choose a scenic route and virtually run through it during their workout.


The Finished workout screen summarizes the workout into calories, steps and course-type (scenic-route). It is summarized in an easy to understand way.

'Congratulations! You deserve a break.' is there to serve as a reminder to users, to workout safely and enjoy their accomplishments.

  • Client: Concept Work
  • Date: March 2015
  • Service: UI, Design

Global Sales Meeting Logo



This logo was created for NanoString's Global Sales Meeting, an annual even that lasts an entire week. The client asked for a logo that represented the coming together of Diagnostics (Prosigna) and Research (NanoString Life Sciences), as well as their global sales representatives from US, EMEA, meeting in one location.


Problem: The previous logo in no way, reflected these goals. The design was outdated and not scaleable. The logo would have to appear on materials such as backpacks, notebooks, pens, and jackets. It needed to have weight that was visible on these mediums.
The final logo shows two halves uniting, NanoString & Prosigna, (represented by the green half-circle), employs a global sales team (the gridded half circle) that will come together in Palm Springs, California. The G in the GSM typeface is rounded and fits inside of the green semi-circle. The 'GSM' text is the same height as the smaller inner circle.
