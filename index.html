PanCancer Campaign & Website



A campaign and website created for an existing product that was part of NanoString's core product line-up. See the website here. All of the artwork, creative, etc. was produced in-house. Creative direction by me, artwork done with Amy Dixon, and web design, front-end development done by me. Planning for the project started in April, production began in March, and the project was finished July. I'm really pleased with how the art turned out and it was an incredible effort to create the entire campaign -- from artwork to design to the actual website -- from scratch.



DRAFT PHASE
Below: Early draft proposal for the PanCancer campaign art. After moodboarding and sketching, we decided to go with an isometric art style that mixed 3D shapes with 2D colors. The workflow, which included an image of an FFPE slide, the nCounter SPRINT instrument, nSolver Software, and PanCancer Panels (the product) were all drawn.
I thought up of the concept, researched with the creative team, and executed the final artwork with Amy Dixon.

Whiteboard sketches of the nSolver artwork. We did hand sketches before digital drawings in order to demonstrate to the client our ideas.

Below: 3D Renderings of each individual piece of artwork (Clockwise: FFPE Slide, nSolver Software, nCounter SPRINT instrument, FFPE Slide - See the biology)

Artistic interpretations of the 3D Renderings (done by Amy D.)

Early drafts of the website design

Final artwork below: PanCancer Assays with cartridge, FFPE slide and solvent.

nSolver Software, which analyzes the results from the PanCancer Assays, once run through the SPRINT Machine.

Assets for the nCouner SPRINT, including samples, 3D Biology (cube), FFPE slide, and a clock (to depict time)

  • Client: NanoString Technologies
  • Date: July 2016
  • Service: Creative Direction, Illustration, Web Design & Development

miRNA Campaign



A product campaign for the mIRNA Expression Assays. miRNA, or MicroRNA, is an exciting area of research because of it's potential links to understanding cancer. Researchers stressed that NanoString's miRNA Expression Assays were more thorough and expansive than it's competitors, and the client requested a new & exciting campaign in order to reflect the product itself.

See the website here .

Art created by Amy Dixon, iconography, website design and subsequent assets created by me. Front-end development was also done by me. The website was planned, proposed and constructed in less than 90 days.

Early mockups of the website before, purple is for media (images or video), and each section is captioned in order to clearly show the flow and content of the website.

  • Client: NanoString
  • Date: August 2016
  • Service: Design, Development

The Domes at UC Davis



GIF Animation of the Domes Front page below.
See the CSS animation in action here


Conceptual design for Baggin's End (The Domes), a co-op housing option for students at UC Davis. I stumbled upon the Domes in my first year, while searching for an open plot in the nearby public gardens. The students who live in the Domes are required to oversee the public gardens, greenhouses and caretake the animals including rabbits, chickens and the Domes mascot cat.
This magical place includes not only the white round domes themselves, but pomegranate trees, grape vines, a yurt, hammocks and outdoor seating recycled from old bicycles, pool chairs and other discarded items. Visitors are always welcome to explore the housing area.

  • Client: Concept Work
  • Date: November 2014
  • Service: CSS Animation, Illustration
